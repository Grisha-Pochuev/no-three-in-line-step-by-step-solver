## Negative results / paths that did not work

1. Single local swaps around the 10-conflict candidate did not improve it.

2. The visible conflict rows alone cannot be repaired while keeping the rest of the board fixed.

3. Direct crop-and-fill attempts from known n=68 configurations did not beat the 10-conflict candidate.

4. Direct reduction from n=70 to n=67 through simple window/crop logic did not produce a solution.

5. A false zero-conflict candidate appeared under a fast but flawed counter. It was rejected by the independent verifier. This is why `tools/verify_few.py` is included here.

## Current interpretation

The n=67 candidate seems to be in a real local trap. The remaining problem is not a tiny one-swap repair. The next useful step is probably a larger constrained reconstruction around the hot rows and columns, or a SAT/CP-style model that fixes most of the board and rebuilds a wider corridor.
